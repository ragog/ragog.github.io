Writing

Sharing an important product of one's mind does not always elicit a positive response. The famous worst examples of that include plenty of people burned at the stake for stating what was deemed contrary to the orthodoxy of their times. But one need not get that close to the grim end of the spectrum, as there are many subtler issues that come with recognition.

Fame, which is heavily sought after, appears to bring with it its fair share of problems. But there might be something subtler still, and yet much more insidious to contend with: by bringing an idea into the public eye, we make it enter a social dimension in which it needs to contend with many things that have nothing to do with its merits.

Many geniuses, intellectuals and in general people with something valuable to say, have successfully brought groundbreaking ideas, art and other creations into that dimension and succeeded in having them influence humanity as they deserved. But the question remains: how many incredible creations of human ingenuity have never left their maker's mind? How many inventive intellects, mindful of the sacrifices they would face in choosing otherwise, decided in favour of the full creative freedom and authenticity that could only be maintained by withholding their idea from global consciousness?
