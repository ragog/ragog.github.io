On average, individuals that have lived in prolonged times of comfort, without getting closely acquainted with existential risks, seemingly tend to a course of action that enables them to maximise the number of opportunities they can take. In the only perspective they have known, limiting losses is seen as a suboptimal strategy, so redundacies are cut. An optimism that is rational only in semblance takes hold, for very rare negative events are easy to discount.

The possibility should be examined that an entirely different set of attitudes and capabilities might be rewarded in an environment where existential risk is frequently and persistently present.

Reacting irrationally to the news when 99% of it is positive seems a recipe for disaster, but is the same true when 99% of the news is negative? To despair when the pantry is full and life merry is surely a bad outcome, but to smile when the pantry is empty and life miserable is entirely different.
